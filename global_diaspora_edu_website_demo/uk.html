Study in UK

Nepali students often struggle to decide: Why study in the UK? To help make their decision easier, it is important that they understand the benefits of a UK education system and what this could mean for their future. The UK offers numerous reasons to study there, especially as an international student, which should be carefully considered before any commitment is made.

Reasons of Choosing Uk

  • Experience the Culture
  • Opportunities for Growth
  • Quality Higher Educations

Why Study in Uk ?

Quality Education and Reputation: UK universities are esteemed for their academic prestige, having held a respected place in global rankings for many years. The courses and programmes offered there provide students with an outstanding education, giving them a solid foundation that can only enhance their future career opportunities. Studying in Britain is truly an experience of excellence.
Multicultural Environment: With a long-standing reputation as an open and welcoming host, the UK is a great place for Nepalis to gain exposure to multiple cultures. As a student in this multicultural nation, you will have enriching opportunities to experience different ways of life, make connections with people from all around the world, and cultivate an outlook that transcends any single culture or country. These experiences can challenge your perspective and give you valuable insight into customs outside of your own.
Scholarships and Funding Opportunities: For students from Nepal who are considering studying in the United Kingdom, there are numerous funding options to support their education. From scholarships and grants to financial aid, UK universities and organisations have a range of opportunities specifically designed for international students. Exploring these awards could make studying in the UK more cost-effective and help reduce any potential burden associated with tuition fees.
Career Opportunities and Employability: With a vibrant job market, international students studying in the UK have access to many fantastic opportunities that can help them get ahead in their careers. A range of internship programmes, part-time roles, and post-study work visas make it possible for students to extend their stay in the UK and gain invaluable experience. Not only are they able to acquire valuable skills during this time, but they also create connections that will give them an edge when applying for jobs across nations.
Rich Cultural and Historical Heritage: As a student studying in Britain, you are presented with a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity to explore its vibrant cultural and historical heritage. By visiting grand landmarks like Buckingham Palace or Big Ben, as well as Stonehenge and other notable sites, you can gain insight into this country's unique past. Furthermore, with theatres and museums strewn across Britain, engaging in cultural activities provides an enriching experience that extends beyond academia.

FAQ

The cost of studying in Australia varies depending on factors such as the chosen institution, course, and location. On average, international students can expect to pay between Bachelor degree GBP 10,000 to GBP 20,000 per year, Master's degree GBP 10,000 to GBP 20,000 per year and Doctoral degree GBP 15,000 to GBP 24,000 per year. Scholarships and part-time work opportunities can help offset some of these costs.

Across the UK there are five stages of education: early years, primary, secondary, Further Education (FE) and Higher Education (HE). Education is compulsory for all children between the ages of 5 (4 in Northern Ireland) and 16. FE is not compulsory and covers non-advanced education which can be taken at further (including tertiary) education colleges and HE institutions (HEIs). The fifth stage, HE, is study beyond GCE A levels and their equivalent which, for most full-time students, takes place in universities and other HEIs and colleges.

International students aiming to study in the UK can secure financial help from a range of scholarships. These awards are made available by universities, government bodies, private foundations, and other sources. Depending on the scholarship programme, they could provide support for tuition fees, cover living costs, or both. To give you an idea of the different offers available, here is a summary along with information about each one's name, value, and eligibility requirements:
University Scholarships: Studying in the United Kingdom is now more accessible to international students due to scholarships offered by several universities. These awards vary, from partial waivers of tuition fees to covering tuition and living costs entirely. To be eligible, applicants may be judged based on their grade point average, demonstrated leadership expertise, or chosen field of study.
Government Scholarships:In order to promote international learning, various UK government bodies, such as the British Council and the UK Foreign and Commonwealth Office, have created scholarships specifically for students from certain countries. These can be single-country or multiple-country awards, giving foreign students a chance to experience higher education in Britain.
External Scholarships: Scholarships for international students studying in the UK are not limited to those sponsored by universities or governments. In fact, numerous external scholarship programs are available, and funded by organisations, foundations, and companies. These scholarships come with particular eligibility requirements that may include academic excellence, financial neediness, or specialised regions of study.
