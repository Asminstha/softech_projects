Study in Japan

Japan is an island country in East Asia. The greatest appeal of studying in Japan is its academic environment where one can study art, technology and acquire the knowledge that enabled Japan's economic growth. Whether it is electronics, Japanese literature, medicine or international business administration, Japanese universities and other institutes of higher education can offer course studies or research programs of virtually any field.

Reasons of Choosing Japan

  • Experience the Culture
  • Opportunities for Growth
  • Quality Higher Educations

Why Study in Japan

Japan is known for providing high-quality education, with some of the world's top-ranking universities. Japanese universities offer world-class research facilities, academic resources, and expert faculty members who are leaders in their fields. Japan is a highly developed country and is known for its technological advancements in various fields such as robotics, engineering, and information technology. As a student, you will have access to cutting-edge technology and research facilities. Japan is known for its unique culture, traditions, and customs, and studying in Japan provides a great opportunity to immerse yourself in this culture. You can experience Japanese arts, cuisine, festivals, and language, which can broaden your horizons and help you become more culturally aware. Japan is one of the safest countries in the world, with a low crime rate and a strong focus on safety and security. This makes it an ideal place for international students to study and live. Japan has a strong economy and a high demand for skilled workers, making it a great place to launch your career after graduation. Additionally, the Japanese government offers several immigration programs that allow international students to stay and work in the country after completing their studies.

FAQ

The cost of studying in Japan can vary widely depending on factors such as the type of institution, the length of study, the program of study, and the student's lifestyle. The cost of tuition and fees can range from around ¥500,000 (US$4,500) to ¥1,000,000 (US$9,000) per year for undergraduate programs, and even higher for graduate programs. Private universities and specialized programs may have higher tuition fees.

The Japanese education system is highly regarded for its academic rigor, discipline, and emphasis on hard work and diligence. It is a 6-3-3-4 system, meaning that students attend six years of elementary school, three years of junior high school, three years of high school, and four years of university or vocational school. University education is highly competitive in Japan, and students must pass entrance exams to gain admission. Universities in Japan are highly regarded for their research and academic excellence. Overall, the Japanese education system is known for its emphasis on academic excellence, discipline, and hard work.

Japan offers a variety of scholarships and funding opportunities for international students.
Monbukagakusho Scholarship: This is a scholarship program offered by the Japanese Ministry of Education, Culture, Sports, Science and Technology (MEXT). The program provides full scholarships to international students who wish to study at Japanese universities, including tuition fees, living expenses, and travel costs.
Japan Student Services Organization (JASSO) Scholarships: JASSO offers several scholarships to international students studying in Japan, such as the Honors Scholarship for Privately Financed International Students, the Student Exchange Support Program (Scholarship for Short-term Study in Japan), and the Follow-up Research Fellowship for Former International Students.
Japan International Cooperation Agency (JICA) Scholarship: JICA provides scholarships for students from developing countries to study at Japanese universities in fields such as agriculture, health, and education.
Private Scholarships:Some private organizations and foundations also offer scholarships for international students studying in Japan, such as the Rotary Yoneyama Memorial Foundation Scholarship, the Toyota Foundation Research Grant Program, and the Nitori International Scholarship Foundation.
University-Specific Scholarships: Many universities in Japan offer scholarships and financial aid to international students based on academic performance, financial need, or a combination of both.
